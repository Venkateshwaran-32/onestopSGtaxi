§ 01 — The Problem

Eight apps. One ride. Nobody wins. Except Grab.

Singapore's ride-hail market just keeps fragmenting — Grab, Gojek, TADA, Ryde, Zig, plus newly-licensed Geolah and Trans-Cab as of Dec 2025. The result: more choice, more confusion, more overpaying.

📱

App Fatigue

Opening 4 apps to compare prices is the unspoken Singapore ride-hailing tax. Surge can shift in the 30 seconds it takes to switch between them. By the time you've decided, the cheapest option is gone.

🎲

Surge Roulette

Grab's algorithm is opaque. Gojek's too. The price you saw at home isn't the price you pay at the kerb. You're guessing — and the platforms profit from that guesswork.

🔒

Loyalty Silos

GrabRewards. Ryde$. TADA tokens. Zig credits. Different currencies, different apps, no way to pool or compare. Points expire. Value evaporates.

🧾

Receipts Scattered

Eight ride-hail apps means eight receipt formats. Try filing a corporate expense report with that. Finance teams call it the "Grab tax" — they spend hours reconciling.

👻

New Entrants Invisible

Geolah and Trans-Cab got full ride-hail licences in December 2025. Most Singaporeans don't know they exist. Smaller operators die unseen — duopoly stays.

⏱️

Timing Blind Spot

What if waiting 12 minutes saves you S$8 because surge is about to ease? You'd never know — no operator has incentive to tell you to wait. The platforms want you to book NOW.

§ 04 — The Arsenal

Every weapon you need. Zero LLM bills.

GAME CHANGERS "Wait, this exists?" — the five reasons people open the app three times a week
The Showstopper

Transit Hopper — live bus combos Maps misses

The next direct bus is in 12 min. But Bus 51 arrives in 2 min and at Buona Vista Bus 111 arrives in 2 min — saving you 12+ min. Google Maps reads the same LTA feed, but optimises for "reliable" over "fastest." We optimise for "fastest *right now*" and surface confidence per leg. 5,200 SG stops, ~400 services, live BusArrivalv2 every search. The first SG-tuned planner that beats the global one.

Cross-app Surge

Wait & Save Coach

A 90-minute price forecast for every taxi operator. Surge isn't synchronized across platforms. When waiting genuinely saves you money, we tell you exactly how long and how much. When it doesn't, we stay quiet. Deterministic surge math, no LLM bills.

The Sticky One

Pin & Watch — real web push

Pin your daily route. Set "anything under S$15." Close the app. Web push fires when surge dips: "TADA dropped to S$13.20." Service worker + VAPID + a 5-min cron poller — production-grade, not a fake "we'll add this later" feature. The retention loop Grab can't copy without admitting their pricing isn't fair.

Reverse Search

Departure Planner

Type a deadline: "Be at Changi by 7pm." The planner walks the forecast backward across every operator and tells you the exact leave-time + cheapest operator that gets you there on time. Trade-offs you can act on, not abstract minimums.

SG-Only Edge

Last-Mile Combo

The trick no operator will build because it cannibalises their fare. "MRT to Clementi + 8-min taxi from there = S$9. Direct taxi = S$23." Layers MRT segments under a final ride-hail leg. Massive savings on long routes from the heartlands.

POWER MOVES The features that turn it from "search tool" into "daily driver"
Real Map

Live Route Map

Pickup pin. Dropoff pin. Polyline. Auto-fits to bounds. Leaflet + free OpenStreetMap tiles — no API keys, no rate limits. Add a Mapbox token and the polyline upgrades to actual road geometry. Feels like Grab, costs nothing.

Group Algo

Trip Splitter (TSP)

One pickup, up to six dropoffs. Brute-force permutation finds the cheapest stop order. The worst order can cost 30% more than the best. CDG Premier and Grab Plus support multi-stop natively — punch in the optimized order, save real money.

Hands-Free

Voice-to-Route

Say "Orchard to Changi". The browser's Web Speech API transcribes locally — no API call, no AI cost. A deterministic parser splits on "to/from" and fuzzy-matches against the SG places list and your recent history.

Expense Layer

Spend Tracker

Every Book tap auto-logs operator, route, and estimated fare. Monthly totals. Per-operator breakdown. Per-trip list. The expense report no operator gives you. Honest disclosure: tracks intended fares, not receipts.

Honest Signal

Forecast Sparkline

A tiny 90-minute trend chart inline with the route info. Trending down = surge easing, wait. Trending up = book now. Glanceable in 200ms. Built on the same forecast engine, surfaced where it's useful.

Receipt Layer

Reverse Compare

After a Grab ride, paste your receipt. We tell you what TADA, Ryde, Zig, Geolah would have charged for the same route at the same time. The "you overpaid" feedback loop that builds the muscle to check before booking, not after.

TABLE STAKES The basics done right — the parts most "MVPs" half-ass

Progressive Web App

"Add to Home Screen" gives you an app icon. Standalone display mode. Manifest, dynamic icons (32/180/192/512/maskable), offline shell. Closer to Grab's iOS app than most native apps are to themselves.

Five Themes

Default, Red & Black, Bumblebee, Ladybug, Raccoon. Theme persists across sessions. Tied to the same shadcn token system, so every new component skins automatically. Try the switcher in the nav bar above.

Privacy-First

Search and compare without signing in. Zustand store + localStorage covers saved routes and history for guests. Sign-in only required to sync across devices (Supabase magic-link). We don't sell data. We never see your real ride.
